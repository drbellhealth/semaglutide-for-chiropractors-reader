How It Works for Real Patients

The flow looks a little different depending on who walks in. Here is how the same model serves different kinds of patients.

These walk-throughs are educational, not medical advice or a promise of results. The point is to show how the referral-and-telemedicine model adapts to the person in front of you, and where the licensed provider's judgment enters the picture in each case.

The Weight-Loss Patient

Consider the patient who simply wants to lose weight. They have tried diets, the scale has not budged, and they trust you because you have treated their back for years. In the program, you do not become their weight-loss doctor; you point them to the branded enrollment page and explain in plain terms what to expect. They fill out the digital intake, get their baseline labs, and have a telemedicine visit with a licensed provider who decides whether semaglutide or tirzepatide is appropriate. If approved, the pharmacy ships the medication to their home and the provider guides the dosing over time. Your contribution is continuity and encouragement: you see them for their regular care, you ask how the program is going, and you help them stay consistent. For many practices, this is the most common scenario, and it is satisfying precisely because you can finally say yes to a request you used to have to turn away.

The Metabolic & Prediabetic Patient

Now picture the patient whose weight is tangled up with early metabolic trouble, the prediabetic numbers, the creeping waistline, the family history they worry about. They are exactly the kind of person who benefits from a licensed provider's careful screening, and the model is built for that. During the telemedicine evaluation, the provider reviews their labs and history, weighs whether a GLP-1 fits their picture, and sets realistic, individualized expectations rather than a one-size promise. If the patient has conditions that need closer attention, the provider may adjust the plan, ask for more information, or decide the medication is not appropriate, which is the system working as intended. Your part stays the same: you refer, you keep the relationship warm, and you reinforce the lifestyle habits that make any weight-loss tool work better. You are not interpreting their labs or managing their metabolism; you are connecting them to a provider who does, and staying their trusted local touchpoint.

Pairing GLP-1s With Hands-On Chiropractic Care

A natural question is how the medication side fits with the hands-on care you already provide, and the honest answer is that they sit side by side rather than blending into one treatment. The GLP-1 program is a distinct medical service managed by the licensed provider; your chiropractic care continues on its own track. What tends to happen in practice is that the two reinforce each other in the patient's experience: as weight comes down, some patients report that movement feels easier and they engage more fully with their adjustment plan, and the regular visits give you natural moments to check in on how the program is going. Keep the lanes clear when you talk to patients, you handle the chiropractic care, the provider handles the medication, and never imply that an adjustment changes a dose or vice versa. Framed honestly, pairing the two is simply offering a patient two helpful things from one practice they already trust, each delivered by the right hands.

When the Right Answer Is to Refer to a Licensed Provider

Sometimes the right answer is to step back and let the licensed provider decide, and recognizing those moments is part of doing this well. Any clinical question, whether a patient is a candidate, what dose to use, how to handle a side effect, whether to continue or stop, belongs to the provider, not to you. If a patient pushes you for a medical opinion, the correct and protective move is to route them to the provider through the program rather than improvise an answer. The same is true if anything feels outside the program's scope or the patient's situation seems complicated; a good partner makes it easy to hand off cleanly. This is not a limitation to apologize for; it is the feature that keeps the whole model legitimate and keeps you out of trouble. Your value is being the trusted referrer and the steady relationship, and knowing when to defer is exactly what makes that role credible to patients and regulators alike.
