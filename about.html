About Semaglutide Program Questions

A note on how this guide was written and what it is for.

This is an educational explainer for chiropractors and practice owners who want a clear, honest picture of how a partner-run GLP-1 weight-loss program actually works. It is written to answer the practical how questions, who prescribes, where the medication comes from, how semaglutide and tirzepatide differ, and what a practice does and does not do, rather than to sell a particular outcome. It is general information, not legal or medical advice.

The model described here keeps prescribing with independent licensed providers and dispensing with a licensed 503A compounding pharmacy, while the practice keeps its brand, pricing, and patient relationship. That separation is the whole point, and it is what makes the arrangement workable for offices without prescriptive authority. Anyone considering a program should confirm the specifics with the partner and with their own advisors before moving forward.

Editorial Approach

We aim to describe this model accurately and independently. We do not invent statistics or promise specific results; GLP-1 outcomes vary by individual and are determined with a licensed provider. Prescribing and dispensing are handled by licensed parties, not the chiropractic practice, and state rules on telemedicine, scope of practice, and marketing vary, so details should always be confirmed locally.
